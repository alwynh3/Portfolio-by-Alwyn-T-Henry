Senior Capstone Project

Environmental Box 2.0

Electronics Lead Sensors PID Tuning

Context: Multiphase Research Lab, Northeastern University

Problem Statement

Design a modular, compact chamber that precisely controls relative humidity (5–95% ±5%) and temperature (20–60 °C ±1 °C), maintains those setpoints for at least 30 minutes, and supports droplet delivery through a dedicated access port, without introducing airflow that perturbs droplet behavior. The chamber must also have multiple viewports to allow cameras to record experiments.

Project Overview

Environmental Box 2.0 is a compact, low‑cost environmental chamber designed to maintain precise temperature and humidity for droplet evaporation experiments. The system achieves research‑grade performance comparable to $18,000+ commercial chambers at a build cost under $1,000.

My Contributions

  • Designed and integrated the electronics architecture (Arduino‑based control system)
  • Selected and validated temperature & humidity sensors (high‑accuracy, fast‑response)
  • Implemented PID control with PWM modulation for temperature regulation
  • Developed humidity control logic with predictive behavior to limit overshoot
  • Tuned PID gains experimentally to achieve ±2.5 °C temperature and ±5% RH stability
  • Assisted with system calibration, testing, and debugging across multiple prototypes

Technical Highlights

  • Dual temperature and humidity control loops
  • Solid‑state relay switching for fast, reliable power modulation
  • Dew‑point protection logic to prevent condensation
  • Stable control maintained for 30+ minutes under sealed conditions

Outcome & Impact

Environmental Box 2.0 now functions as a reconfigurable research instrument used for reproducible droplet experiments, significantly improving performance over Environmental Box 1.0.

Cornerstone Design Project

Carbon Monoxide Sensing Bird

CAD Design Lead System Integration

Project Concept

Inspired by historical mining safety practices, this project re‑imagines the canary‑in‑a‑coal‑mine using modern sensing technology. We designed a bird that visually and mechanically responds to dangerous carbon monoxide (CO) levels.

System Behavior

  • CO sensor continuously monitors ambient concentration
  • When CO exceeds a harmful threshold:
    • An LED indicator activates
    • A motor lowers the bird’s position, symbolizing danger

My Contributions

  • Designed the mechanical structure and housing in CAD
  • Developed component layouts for motor and linkage integration
  • Supported sensor‑motor interaction logic and physical assembly
  • Ensured manufacturability and clean motion of the lowering mechanism

Why It Matters

This project combines engineering, storytelling, and safety, translating invisible hazards into intuitive physical feedback.

Mechanical Design Project

Scissor Lift Shopping Cart

Analysis Lead Force Calculations

Project Overview

An assistive grocery shopping cart designed to help users with limited mobility safely lift and load groceries.

My Contributions

  • Performed free‑body diagrams (FBDs) for worst‑case loading scenarios
  • Designed and analyzed the ACME power screw system
  • Calculated:
    • Required lifting torque
    • Mechanical efficiency
    • Motor power requirements
  • Supported component sizing decisions using hand calculations + FEA results

Key Results

  • Double‑threaded 1‑in ACME power screw
  • ~0.083 kip·in required torque
  • ~0.063 hp minimum motor power
  • High safety factors across scissor arms, pins, welds, and fasteners

Engineering Focus

This project emphasized design for safety, validation through analysis, and translating theoretical mechanics into a practical, user‑centered product.

ACE Program

Site Planning & Architectural Visualization

Site Planning SketchUp

Project Overview

As part of the ACE (Architecture, Construction, Engineering) Program, I contributed to the early-stage planning and visualization of a residential outdoor space, transforming an undeveloped site into a functional layout.

My Contributions
